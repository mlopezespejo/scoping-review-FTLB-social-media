# Social Media Exposure and Functional Tic-Like Behaviors in Adolescents: A Scoping Review

## Overview

This repository contains the protocol and supporting materials for a scoping review examining the relationship between exposure to tic- or Tourette-related social media content and the onset, exacerbation, or persistence of functional tic-like behaviors (FTLB) in adolescents and young adults during and after the COVID-19 pandemic.

The review is conducted in accordance with [JBI scoping review methodology](https://synthesismanual.jbi.global) and reported using [PRISMA-ScR](https://doi.org/10.7326/M18-0850) guidelines.

## Review Questions

1. What evidence exists from 2020 onward on the relationship between exposure to tic- or Tourette-related social media content and the onset, exacerbation, or persistence of FTLB in adolescents and young adults?
2. What methods have been used to measure exposure and to support inferences about observational learning, behavioral modeling, reinforcement, or sociogenic contagion?
